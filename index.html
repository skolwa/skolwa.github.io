Sthabile Kolwa, PhD

About

From January 2021, I have been an Astronomer at the Physics Faculty of the University of Johannesburg specialising in the statistical and phenomenological study of nearby and distant radio galaxies.

Astronomer & Lecturer

My research in Astrophysics involves using observational data sourced from radio, optical, mm/sub-mm and infrared telescopes - both ground and space-based to study causality between the properties of radio-loud galaxies hosting active galactic nuclei (AGN) and their kpc to Mpc scale environments.

As a lecturer, I have recently finsihed teaching a third year Astrophysics course at the University of Cape Town. In 2021, I will take over as a co-lecturer for engineering and physics undergraduate students.


As an early-career Astronomer, I have performed numerous analyses on VLA, Hubble, Spitzer, MUSE and ALMA datasets. For this, I have developed customised software pipelines in Python to produce results detailing properties of radio-loud AGN such as surface brightness, kinematics, mass, cluster and group scale density (see my GitHub). As a result, I am proficient in the process of reducing optical integral field unit (IFU) data as well as calibrating and imaging interferometer data. The scientific results of my work during the past five years have been published in the journals, Monthly Notices for the Royal Astronomical Society and Astronomy & Astrophysics. Please see ORCID for listing of my published works which are also listed in the Publications section below.

Currently, my focus is on geared towards radio window. My goal is to make use of MeerKAT continuum surveys (MIGHTEE), upgraded GMRT / superMIGHTEE and ALMA data to constrain the radio properties of sources in the XMM-LSS fields. This will involve statistical measures of sample-wide spectral indices and spectral energy distribution fitting. I am interested in using these new and more sensitive radio datasets and the methods described to provide reasonable constraints on the physics underlying the formation, launching and propagation of jets in radio galaxy cores.

I like to think about what solutions can be made to improve STEM education in South Africa at the school and university level. I see this as fundamental to the forward progression of a technologically sophisicated and globally competitive nation. I'll be heavily involved in Outreach projects that aim to plan and implement projects that will bring about a positive change in this regard.

When I'm not uncovering mysteries of the Universe, I am run long distance and occasionally register for races. With my love for music, I have developed into a novice but keen guitarist, MIDI control beat-maker & vocalist. I also enjoy a regular PC game or two. I find the smell of freshly printed books delicious and live to get lost in a good sci-fi, fantasy, graphic novel, YA, yaoi manga, memoir or popular science book. Hence, I'm a bibliophile of sorts.
